What is it?

Digital Health Literacy is the ability to seek, find, understand, and appraise health information from electronic sources — and use it to make smart decisions about your health.

Need to know 👀

5 principles.

The rules for finding real health info in a world full of noise.

01→

Verify Before You Share

Cross-check everything against WHO, CDC, or peer-reviewed sources before hitting that share button.

02→

Check the Source

Who wrote it? What are their credentials? Does the site have real editorial standards? Ask every time.

03

Spot Misinformation

Sensational headlines, zero citations, anecdotal-only evidence — your red flags for bad health info.

03
04→

Guard Your Data

Health apps collect personal info. Read the privacy policy — boring, but your data is worth protecting.

05→

Use Trusted Platforms

Go to government health portals, accredited hospitals, and pro medical organizations for your questions.
